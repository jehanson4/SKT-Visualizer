The Science Behind the Pictures

Q: What am I looking at?

A: You are looking at the configuration space of an N-dimensional SK model in which the energy surface consists of two isotropic potential wells centered on two distinct points p1 and p2. Locations on the hemispherical Surface represent equivalence classes of points in the configuration space, with each equivalence class located at the crossing of two lines in the Net. The equivalence classes are defined in terms of distance in configuration space: all points in a given equivalence class are k1 steps from p1 and k2 steps from p2. The two equivalence classes respectively containing p1 and p2 themselves are located at the feet of the two Meridians.

Four numbers are sufficient to define the fundamental properties of the model:

  • N, the dimensionality of the SK model
  • k0, the distance between p1 and p2
  • α1, the depth of the potential well centered on p1
  • α2, the depth of the well centered on p2
The thermodynamic properties also depend on:
  • T, temperature (alternatively expressed in terms of β ≡ 1/T)

The Science Behind the Pictures

Q: What am I looking at?

A: You are looking at an N-dimensional SK model in which the energy surface consists of two isotropic potential wells centered on two distinct points p1 and p2. Points on the hemispherical shell shown in the pictures represent equivalence classes of the points in the SK model's configuration space. The equivalence classes are defined in terms of distance in configuration space: all points in a given equivalence class are k1 steps from p1 and k2 steps from p2. The two equivalence classes respectively containing p1 and p2 themselves are located on the shell's equator.

Let's unpack that.

Four numbers are sufficient to define the fundamental properties of the model:

  • N, the dimensionality of the SK model
  • k0, the distance between p1 and p2
  • α1, the depth of the potential well centered on p1
  • α2, the depth of the well centered on p2
The thermodynamic properties also depend on:
  • T, temperature (alternatively expressed in terms of β ≡ 1/T)

In general, the configuration space of a system is the space of all possible configurations of that system. Each degree of freedom in the system is assigned its own dimension in configuration space. Each possible configuration is a distinct point in the space.

For example: a light-switch has two possible configurations "off" and "on"; its configuration space is a 1-dimensional space containing two points. A system consisting of two light-switches has two degrees of freedom and four possible configurations: [off, off], [off, on], [on, off], [on, on]; its configuration space is a 2-dimensional space containing four points. A system of N light-switches has an N-dimensional configuration space containing 2^N points.

Q: What was I just looking at?

A: You were looking at an N-dimensional SK model embedded in a hemispherical shell. The model's energy surface consists of two isotropic potential wells centered on two arbitrarily chosen distinguished points p1 and p2. Locations on the hemispherical shell represent equivalence classes of the points in the SK model's configuration space, defined such that all points in a given equivalence class are at Hamming distance k1 from p1 and k2 from p2. The embedding preserves these distances. The two equivalence classes respectively containing p1 and p2 themselves are located on the hemisphere's equator, while equivalence classes located up by the pole are approximately distance N/2 from both p1 and p2.
